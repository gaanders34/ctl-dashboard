          Sales, Inventory & Operations Planning (SIOP)

          Align demand, supply, and capacity in one view. Use the sections below to paste or enter data, then review the plan vs actual and recommendations.

          What you need to build out SIOP

          • Demand — A demand plan or forecast (e.g. by week or month): coils, tons, or $ by product/customer. Paste from Excel or enter manually. You may already have order backlog from Open Status; SIOP adds a forward-looking demand view.
          • Supply / Capacity — Planned or actual production (coils, tons) by period. You can roll up from your Production Schedule or from the Dashboard MTD/forecast. Paste by week or month to compare to demand.
          • Inventory (optional) — On-hand or in-transit inventory by SKU or product family. Helps show “days of supply” and whether to build ahead or pull in orders.
          • Time buckets — Decide the planning horizon: weekly (e.g. next 4–8 weeks) or monthly (e.g. next 3–6 months). Same buckets for demand and supply so you can match them.
          • Demand vs Supply table — A simple grid: Period | Demand | Supply | Gap | Cumulative gap. Gap = Demand − Supply; cumulative gap shows whether you’re building backlog or surplus over time.
          • Recommendations / actions — Logic or notes such as: “Increase production in Week X,” “Pull forward orders,” “Expedite material,” based on gap and inventory. Can be manual at first, then rules later.
          • Persistence — Like OTD Tracker, store SIOP data in the browser (localStorage) so you can compare week-over-week or month-over-month and track plan vs actual over time.

          Next steps: add paste areas for Demand and Supply (with headers like Period, Demand, Supply), a table that computes Gap and Cumulative Gap, and an optional “Recommendations” list. You can later add charts (demand vs supply by period) and link to your existing Production Schedule and Open Status data.
